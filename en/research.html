Research

Learning controllers with verifiable probability guarantees.

I study reach-avoid control for stochastic systems: guiding the state toward a target set, keeping it away from unsafe regions, and certifying a formal lower bound on the success probability.

View pipeline Resume & materials
Problem setting

Start with a precise specification.

The reach-avoid task combines two requirements under random disturbance: reach the target set and avoid unsafe regions, with the conclusion expressed as a certifiable probability lower bound.

Sets and requirement

Fix the initial set, target set, unsafe set, and probability threshold.

Stochastic dynamics

Study how random disturbance affects safety and reachability in discrete time.

Candidate controller

Learn a usable reference policy before moving into certification.

Probability guarantee

Use certificates and optimization to produce a lower bound, not just simulation traces.

Method chain

Current pipeline

1. Specify the task

Fix the initial set, target set, unsafe set, and probability threshold.

2. Learn a reference policy

Use SAC to train a candidate controller as a workable starting point.

3. Build a PAC polynomial approximation

Convert the learned policy into a polynomial controller for certification.

4. Solve for certificates and bounds

Combine stochastic barrier-like certificates with SOS / SDP to obtain a formal lower bound.

5. Iterate the controller or certificate

If the result is conservative, update the controller, certificate template, or approximation setup.

Current focus

The focus is an iterative guarantee.

  • Learning, approximation, certification, and updates should feed into one another.
  • Alongside the reach-avoid probability bound, I track controller degree, certificate degree, and solver cost.
  • The aim is a repeatable safe-control workflow, not only one good-looking simulation.
Relation

How this connects to verification work

Many verification workflows begin with a fixed controller and ask whether it satisfies a high-probability reach-avoid specification. My current work starts one step earlier: the controller is learned first, converted into a certifiable form, and then passed through certificate solving and updates.

Output

A trace for each iteration

Each round should record not only a probability lower bound, but also controller degree, certificate degree, sample settings, solver time, and failure reasons. That makes the next update concrete.
