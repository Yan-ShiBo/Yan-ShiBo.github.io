Turning stochastic-system safety into verifiable engineering capability

I am currently pursuing an M.Eng. in Software Engineering at Southwest University. My work focuses on formal methods and safety verification for stochastic control systems, with particular interests in reach-avoid specifications, RL-aided controller synthesis, stochastic barrier-like certificates, PAC approximation, and SOS optimization. The broader goal is to move safety from heuristic design toward workflows that are evidence-driven, computationally grounded, and reproducible.

Research Focus

The problem I most want to solve

The point is not merely to make controllers work, but to make them interpretable and verifiable under stochasticity, uncertainty, and safety constraints.

Safe control of stochastic systems

I study how safety constraints can still be satisfied in the presence of noise, disturbance, and uncertainty, especially under reach-avoid specifications.

Formal methods and post-verification

By combining barrier-like certificates, PAC approximation, and SOS optimization, I aim to turn “working in practice” into “provable in theory”.

Learning-aided synthesis

Reinforcement learning can be used for policy search, while formal verification provides the safety backstop needed for high-stakes deployment.

Method Pipeline

Current methodological chain

This pipeline showcases the technical identity of the site far better than a generic widget or clock.

1. Model the system

Start from nonlinear stochastic dynamics and safety specifications to define a precise reach-avoid control objective.

2. Learn candidate policies

Use learning-based or approximate methods to search for high-quality control policies in complex settings.

3. Construct certificates

Build stochastic barrier-like certificates so that empirical success can be connected to a formal proof object.

4. Optimize computationally

Leverage PAC polynomial approximation and sum-of-squares optimization to make verification workflows computable and reproducible.

5. Deliver guarantees

The outcome is a control policy with formal safety guarantees for safety-critical stochastic systems.
